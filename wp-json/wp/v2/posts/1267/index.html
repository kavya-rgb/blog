TLDR at the bottom.<\/p>\n

I’m a full stack web dev and work full-time, making a decent living, but of course there’s still room for improvement.<\/p>\n

Over the years I’ve consistently and constantly been launching different kind of personal projects, but none of them brought any significant income, long story short, in chronological order:<\/p>\n

– Started selling premium WordPress themes, one of them got accepted on ThemeForest, made 400$, but received about 80$ due to all commissions… Wasted about 9 months on it.<\/p>\n

– Started creating content sites hoping for AdSense, but they pretty much failed (later tried again, see below)<\/p>\n

– Created a very very complex ride-sharing site for our local market, had even more features than BlaBlaCar, but it never was successful, nor was I able to monetize it due to our laws… Aaaand it was in a grey legal area also, so I’ve abandoned it and only keep it in my portfolio to show off.<\/p>\n

– Tried a couple more themes – couldn’t get them accepted, wasted some more months.<\/p>\n

– Set up a new content-focused site, on a subject which I’m passionate about – local tourism, it became the most popular site in the country (on this topic), but I can barely cover hosting costs from it (I get about 5-6k monthly visitors)<\/p>\n

– Launched a similar site, but for UK – still a long way to go. Getting on top for some search queries, but still not even accepted to AdSense.<\/p>\n

– Have a couple of almost-ready games and apps ready to launch, but can’t finish them, feel unmotivated.<\/p>\n

– Created a Volkswagen parts dropshipping site – it started to get popular thank to organic search, but no sales yet.<\/p>\n

– Created a WordPress plugin that works like YouTube shorts – got rejected on CodeCanyon…<\/p>\n

At this point I’d probably make much more just doing WordPress freelance, but I just don’t want to work like this, I want some of my personal projects to start bringing in money… But I guess I have to work much much harder than that…<\/p>\n

TLDR: Launched dozens of personal projects, none of them successful in terms of income.<\/p>\n
