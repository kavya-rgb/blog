    \n <\/a>
    \n > Can’t be Great at everything!
    \n<\/h2>\n

    <\/a><\/p>\n

    Yes, this is the biggest mistake I have made, and still struggling with it. People who have several roles and responsibilities in their lives initially consider excelling at each of them. This seems over-motivated and under-evaluated.
    Pick your battles wisely<\/em>.
    Input time as per the value of the outcome the task will yield.<\/p>\n

    \n
    \n <\/a>
    \n > Priority List
    \n<\/h2>\n

    <\/a><\/p>\n

    I know it seems overburdened right now. There are multiple tasks on your to-do list.
    Rather than a to-do list, I keep a mental priority list. The most important stuff has to be done first. I know, sounds like duh!!<\/em> but the problem is that we forget the difference important<\/u> and urgent<\/u> tasks.
    Thanks to     Harsh Mishra<\/a> I came to know about something called the EISENHOWER MATRIX<\/a><\/strong>. I am greatly grateful to him for introducing this to me.<\/p>\n

    \n
    \n <\/a>
    \n > Have a Zen time
    \n<\/h2>\n

    <\/a><\/p>\n

    Don’t let internships, colleges, or anyone else interfere with your time alone. Just you and your thoughts. It helps you plan the day more effectively. Establishes mental peace and also stepCount++.<\/p>\n

    \n > Burnout is Real
    \n<\/h2>\n

    <\/a><\/p>\n

    Yes! I am no robot. I too get tired and feel like “Sabb moh Maaya h” (everything is meaningless). You need to take breaks too!
