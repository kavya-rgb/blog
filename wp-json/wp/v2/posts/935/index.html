My biggest technical goals are\u2026
\n<\/h2>\n

continuing to develop my systems thinking I want to broaden my toolkit of problem-solving approaches and approach issues from a variety of informed perspectives before implementing a solution.<\/p>\n

On a similar note, I’d like to give some great tech talks this year and devote more time to writing. Even if you take the teacher out of the classroom, I still enjoy sharing interesting ideas and concepts with anyone who will listen.<\/p>\n

\n

My biggest technical achievements are\u2026
\n<\/h2>\n

On a professional level, I’m proud to have progressed to the point in my career where I’m known as a great problem solver. Many coworkers have told me that I’m great at being thrown into problems and coming up with creative solutions rather than just coding them up. I’ve also had many former teammates and even managers tell me that they learned a lot from working with me. I’m humbled to know that there are many people who would gladly work with me again (and have even tried to drag me to their jobs with them)!<\/p>\n

\n

I pledge to break the bias in tech by\u2026
\n<\/h2>\n

I try not only to shine as brightly as I can in the tech industry, but also to help other women I meet shine as well. When people assume that women aren’t technical or that they can’t work at the same level as men in the same settings, I believe they’re missing out on the creativity, intelligence, and empathy that their products and teams can have. It’s not always easy to demonstrate this, but by continuing to do so, whether through my own work or by highlighting the work of other women, I’m helping to improve the tech industry as a whole.<\/p>\n

\n

I\u2019m excited about\u2026
\n<\/h2>\n

I’m about to embark on my first-ever digital nomad journey. I’m looking forward to the challenge of traveling and working in different countries. Also, a chance to start a van life and adapt to a new way of life.<\/p>\n

Thank you to DEV and GitHub for their generous donations for #SheCoded posts! Let us continue to create opportunities for and support women and girls in technology.\n<\/p>\n
