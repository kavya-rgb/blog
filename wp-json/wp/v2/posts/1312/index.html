I know, it\u2019s probably been asked, but I can\u2019t find it anywhere. I have a degree unrelated to CS but taught myself how to program using some online resources and built tons of stuff. I freelanced for a year and did two decently sized projects. One was client working for an e-commerce site that was doing 1mil+ a year in sales. After that, I picked up some smaller gigs and eventually interviewed a bunch until I landed my current job working on a React app for a local company. <\/p>\n

Before I got the job I got accepted into a post-bacc program that I wanted to do because I was having so much trouble landing my first job. Now that I\u2019m working in the field, it feels foolish to spend my money on another degree where I\u2019m basically re-learning what I already know or filling in knowledge gaps (which is good but I can do this on my own). <\/p>\n

I guess my question is this: is it worth it to spend the money and time acquiring a CS degree over the next two years if, by then, I\u2019ll have nearly 3 years exp at jobs and a year of experience freelancing? <\/p>\n
